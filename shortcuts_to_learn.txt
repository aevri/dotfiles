Bash:

<CTRL-a>  # beginning of line
<CTRL-e>  # end of line
<ALT-b>   # back one word
<ALT-f>   # forward one word
<CTRL-xx> # toggle between cursor pos and beginning of line
<CTRL-u>  # cut before the cursor to beginning of line
<CTRL-k>  # cut after the cursor to end of line
<CTRL-w>  # cut the word before the cursor
<ALT-d>   # cut the word after the cursor
<CTRL-xe> # edit the current line with $EDITOR and execute result on exit

Source: http://ss64.com/bash/syntax-keyboard.html

GNU Screen:

<CTRL-ad> # detach the current screen from the terminal
